KRAS codon 12/13 mutations are found in approximately 10-30% lung adenocarcinomas and associated with history of smoking (1). EGFR exon 19 and 21 mutations are present in about 10% of [Redacted]s and up to 40% in the [Redacted] population, common in non-smokers and associated with the tumor response to treatment with EGFR inhibitors (2).

Clinical studies show high EGFR gene copy number by FISH analysis (amplification) to be associated with favorable clinical benefit (clinical response, stable disease, time to progression, and survival) in patients with advanced non-small cell lung cancer treated with EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors.
