1. UTERUS, CERVIX, BILATERAL OVARIES AND FALLOPIAN TUBES; TOTAL HYSTERECTOMY AND BILATERAL 
SALPINGO-OOPHORECTOMY:
- IMMUNOHISTOCHEMICAL STAINS SHOW APPARENT LOSS OF MSH6 PROTEIN, ALTHOUGH IT IS FOCALLY DIFFICULT TO DISTINGUISH STAINING IN STROMAL ELEMENTS VERSUS RARE TUMOR CELLS. MLH1 AND MSH2 PROTEINS SHOW INTACT EXPRESSION.
- THE LOSS OF EXPRESSION OF MSH6, ALTHOUGH NOT DIAGNOSTIC, MAY SIGNIFY THE PRESENCE OF
MICROSATELLITE INSTABILITY IN THIS PATIENT.
- PLEASE CORRELATE WITH GENETIC TESTING.
